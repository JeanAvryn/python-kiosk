School History

Dr. Ramon de Santos National High School Legacy

Our Foundation

It was founded in July 1966 through a municipal council of Cuyapo. The first petitioners on the grant of the establishment of the school were a group of elementary teachers of San Antonio Elementary School led by Severina Santero, Crispino Pineda, Leoncio Meemita, Arnulfo Mangabat Sr., Purificacion Evangelista, Emilio Jose, Magdalena Jose, and Petra Gajeton who had filed the resolution of the then 1st secondary school in Cuyapo Municipal Council.

Our Namesake

The school lot was donated by Pochola de Santos, daughter of the late Dr. Ramon de Santos, the lot owner. The school was named after the landowner's name covering one hectare of the lot adjacent to San Antonio Elementary School where it was housed for twenty-two years.

Milestones

1966

School Founding

Dr. Ramon de Santos Barangay High School opened with 56 first-year students and 30 second-year students coming from Cuyapo and Guimba.

1984

Newly Nationalized High School

The school was officially recognized as a nationalized high school in 1984 based on the Batas Pambansa Blg. 639.

2000s

Opening of Special Programs

The school began offering special programs such as Science, Technology, and Engineering (STE), and Special Program in Information and Communications Technology (SP-ICT).

2016

Opening of Senior High School

The school officially opened its Senior High School program in 2016-2017.

2026

RaMonitor Launch

The RaMonitor system was launched in 2024 to enhance student monitoring and academic performance tracking.
